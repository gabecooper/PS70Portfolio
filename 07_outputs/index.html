Week 7: Electronic Outputs

Assignment: Minimum Viable Product for Final Project

For my MVP, I decided that I wanted to see each mechanical element of my project work as intended. To do this, I chose to control the scoreboard using potentiometers instead of a Bluetooth message. Here was my initial plan:

There were three main areas to consider: the elevator, the emptier, and the dropper.

First, the elevator. I explored several ideas initially: a conveyor belt, then a chain-style system, and even a cam-and-follower “stair” mechanism.

In the end, I chose a hydraulic screw setup because it was the most compact and reliable. 3D-printing required careful measurement of the marble, but the process ended up being quick.

The main challenge was allowing the marble to dismount easily while still keeping it “protected” inside the elevator. My initial design was fully vertical and used dowels to prevent the marble from falling out. However, after struggling with consistent loading and unloading, I pitched the screw at a 45° angle and used gravity to keep the marble in place. This change worked well. The elevator is now my favorite part of the project and is satisfying to watch.

Second, the dropper. I chose a Scotch yoke mechanism to create translational motion because I wanted to use a servo, keep the design compact, and make it visually interesting.

However, I’ve run into torque limitations, so I’m considering switching to a rack-and-pinion system in future iterations. The Scotch yoke pulls a laser-cut tab out from under the marbles, releasing them down a slide—similar to the mechanic used in Connect 4.

Finally, the dropper. The idea is that the elevator deposits a marble (via a tube) into the dropper, which then precisely places it into a selected column based on microcontroller input.

To simplify alignment since the final dropper will sit centrally I designed the columns to be lower toward the middle. This allows a dropper of constant diameter to reach each column equally. In this MVP, the dropper is controlled directly by a potentiometer (with smoothing applied due to noisy readings). 


===CODE=== 
int angle1 = constrain(pot1.getSteppedAngle(), 0, 180);
int angle2 = constrain(pot2.getSteppedAngle(), 0, 180);
servo1.writeAngle(angle1);
servo2.writeAngle(angle2);

Going forward:

  • Expand from 4 columns to 8
  • Integrate everything into a single, uniform frame with dedicated marble pathways
  • Replace the Scotch yoke with a rack-and-pinion system (still servo-driven)
  • Switch from potentiometer control to Bluetooth

Where I could use help:

  • There is significant friction in the dropper. Would using two rack-and-pinion systems help, or should I try reducing friction through straigtening it out or adding grease?
  • Given that I'm removing potentiometers, I was hoping to add another sensor. Presently my idea is to add a photoresistor at the top to detect when a marble enters the dropper, though a capacitive sensor could also work. Anyone have other sensor ideas?
  • What is the best way to ensure consistent timing and spacing between marbles as they move from the elevator into the dropper?
