Week 3: Hand Tools and Fabrication

Kinetic Sculpture

For my Kinetic sculpture, I wanted to make a marble conveyor belt. This ended up taking way, way more time than I expected. The easiest step was following the tutorial on the PHYSCI70 website to create housing for the motor. I ended up using the front pieces as a means of attaching the motor to the knob on my conveyor belt.

One challenge I had was selecting what material to use for the conveyor belt. In the end, like with real conveyor belts, I choose to use rubber. And, according to thistothat.com, I used Barge as an adhesive when I had to connect two ends of rubber together so that the length of my band was long enough. Likewise, I used Barge to attach wooden dowels to the rubber to hold the marbles as the motors turn. Another thing that was exciting to me was the idea that the system be closed. Specifically, that the marble gets returned from the top of the conveyor belt to the bottom. For that, I made a paper roller coaster. To attach the motor, I fitted it first with the cardboard piece from the tutorial and glued the cardboard piece to the wood dowel. The other dowel, I decided would be allow to spin freely. However, the first time I ran the machine, the motor was spinning really fast and broke the conveyor belt. Hence, for my final version, I decided to fit the motor with a gear and the spinning dowel with another gear (forming a 3:1 ratio) such that the dowel would spin a lot slower then the motor with more power. This aligns more with my goal of keeping the conveyor belt steady but functional.
